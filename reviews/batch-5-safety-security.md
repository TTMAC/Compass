## Fixes Applied

### Forbidden-term sweep (B-level)

- **ss-1-1** line 84: "authorised at the highest levels of government" → "authorised at the highest reaches of the apartheid state". Restores domain language without losing the historical claim about the Security Branch's operational authorisation.
- **ss-1-1** line 140: "no alternative policing tier that can compensate for failure at the centre" → "no alternative policing sphere that can compensate for failure at the centre". Note that "tier" remains acceptable elsewhere in the corpus where it names the proposed reform model (the three-tier policing architecture in ss-3-1); the discipline is to avoid "tier" as a sphere-synonym in the abstract.
- **ss-3-1** line 74: APCOF expert block "distributed across multiple levels of government" → "distributed across multiple spheres of government". The agent observation that the editor — not the expert — is responsible for domain-language consistency in expert blocks is correct.

### Evidentiary precision (B-level)

- **ss-2-3** lines 39 and 49: remand-population claim and 200% capacity statistic now explicitly anchored to the Judicial Inspectorate for Correctional Services and the DCS Annual Report 2022/23.

Prevents the figure from being cited downstream as if it were a single empirically validated rate.
- **ss-3-5** line 36: 40% recidivism rate now cites DCS Annual Report figures alongside NICRO and CSVR academic studies, with an explicit 40–55% range depending on cohort and follow-up period.

- **ss-3-5** line 56: facility-population figure now anchored to the DCS Annual Report 2022/23.

- **ss-4-2** line 103: R30–47 billion reform-cost range now carries an explicit derivation note ("bottom-up estimates derived by aggregating the phase-by-phase cost projections set out above"), with named risk dimensions (procurement, exchange-rate variation, reallocation pace).

### Partisan-drift discipline
