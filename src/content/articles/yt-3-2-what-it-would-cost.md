## The Bottom Line, Up Front

A serious proposal shows its arithmetic. This article builds the cost from the bottom up — per participant, then per phase — with every assumption on the table. (The *funding* of that cost, and the scorecard, follow in the next article.)

## Outline

- **BLUF / hook** — Lead with a single transparent per-participant number range and the promise to show every assumption behind it.
- **Why bottom-up** — Top-down "it'll cost billions" numbers are useless for decision-making; build from the unit cost up so the figure can be interrogated and pressure-tested.
- **The cost components (per participant, per year)** — Stipend; basic-training cost (the universal Stage-1 phase from Article 2.1, including SANDF training capacity); deployment-phase costs by track (education/health/infrastructure supervision); uniforms/equipment; placement and supervision; administration and systems. Show a worked illustrative per-participant-year figure as a stated rand range — *all assumptions explicit*.
- **Cost by phase** — Multiply per-participant cost by the Phase 1/2/3 cohort sizes from the rollout article to get indicative annual totals; show the budget ramp from a modest pilot to a near-universal cohort.
- **Sensitivity** — How the total moves with the big levers: stipend level, training-to-deployment duration split, cohort size, track mix. Give a range, not false precision.
- **What's expensive, what isn't** — Where the money actually goes (mostly stipends at scale); implications for design choices.
- **Reader's struggling moment** — "These numbers can't be real." Show the workings so the reader can check them.
- **Close with the practical tool** — A transparent per-participant costing worksheet the reader can adjust — handing off to the funding question in the next article.
