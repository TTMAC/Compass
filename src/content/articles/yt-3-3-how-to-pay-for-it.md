## The Bottom Line, Up Front

The previous article priced the programme; this one funds it and judges it. Funding comes mainly from consolidating money South Africa already spends on fragmented youth schemes, complemented by the skills levy and phased allocation — and every rand is tied to a published scorecard.

## Outline

- **BLUF / hook** — Lead on the honest reframe: much of the money already exists, scattered across overlapping programmes; the task is consolidation and sequencing, not only new spending.
- **Funding options (honest, non-partisan, with trade-offs)**
  1. **Reprioritise and consolidate** — fold fragmented youth/EPWP/learnership budgets (NYDA, EPWP, CWP, PYEI, NARYSEC) into one coherent envelope; show the indicative pool.
  2. **The skills levy (SETA) system** — repurposing a share of existing skills-development funding toward the civilian-track skilling.
  3. **Phased fiscal allocation** — new allocation that ramps with the phases (small in pilot, larger at scale), protecting the early budget.
  4. **Partnership / DFI co-funding** — private CSI and development-finance co-funding specifically for the venture-finance facility from Article 2.3.
  - Be explicit about what is *not* affordable and what must be traded off; respect the fiscal reality covered in the Economy pillar.
- **The cost–benefit frame** — Offsets: services delivered (classrooms supported, clinics staffed, infrastructure built/maintained), reduced youth-unemployment costs, tax-base and crime-reduction effects, the stipend multiplier. Cite ranges; avoid over-claiming.
- **Measurement and accountability (the scorecard)** — Placement/completion rates; post-service employment and enterprise-creation rates; cohesion indicators; cost-per-outcome; audit outcomes. Tie to DPME-style evaluation and the Reform Agenda's measurement discipline. Publish it.
- **Guarding the money** — Anti-capture controls on procurement, stipends, and the finance facility (cross-link to Reform Agenda anti-capture architecture).
- **Reader's struggling moment** — "We can't afford this." Engage directly: phasing keeps early cost modest while evidence accumulates, and consolidation means much is reallocation, not pure new spend.
- **Close with the practical tool** — A funding-and-scorecard one-pager that citizens and legislators can interrogate.
