## The Bottom Line, Up Front

Big programmes fail when they start big. The proposal is a three-phase path — prove it, scale it, then make it universal — each phase gated by evidence, with the institutional and legal architecture built deliberately rather than improvised.

## Outline

- **BLUF / hook** — State the phasing in one line, then make the case that sequencing *is* the policy.
- **Phase 1 — Prove it (pilot, ~2–3 years)** — A volunteer cohort in 1–2 provinces, ~10,000–20,000 participants across both tracks; tight M&E; deliberately over-resourced to learn. Define success criteria up front.
- **Phase 2 — Scale it (national, voluntary-plus-incentive, ~3–5 years)** — National roll-out at ~100,000–200,000 per cohort; voluntary but with the Part 2 benefits making it the obvious choice; build delivery capacity.
- **Phase 3 — Universalise it (legislated, near-universal)** — Statutory near-universal service per cohort (~500,000+); the legitimacy and prestige earned in earlier phases make compulsion acceptable. Be explicit about constitutional and rights considerations (freedom of trade/occupation, equality) and how a civilian track addresses conscientious objection.
- **Institutional architecture** — Who runs it: a dedicated national service authority vs. an inter-departmental model (Defence + Basic Education + Health + Public Works + DPME). Governance, delivery partners (TVETs, provinces, municipalities, NGOs), data systems. Lessons from the Reform Agenda on authority–accountability alignment.
- **Legislative pathway** — Enabling Act; amendments touching defence, education-employment, public-works frameworks; the sequencing of law to match the phases.
- **Risk register** — Capture, fiscal slippage, safety/safeguarding of young people, quality dilution at scale, civil-military boundaries. Mitigations for each.
- **Reader's struggling moment** — "Government can't run anything this big." Address candidly via phasing, gating, and capacity-building.
- **Close with the practical tool** — A phase-gate roadmap (timeline with go/no-go criteria).
