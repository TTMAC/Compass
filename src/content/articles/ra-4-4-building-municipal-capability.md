## The Reform Blueprint for Information

:::framework
**The Municipal Capability Audit Framework**

Use these five tests to assess whether your municipality has the capability and information systems to deliver services — before a crisis forces the question:

**Test 1: The professional appointments test.**

Check your municipality's annual report for Section 56 and Section 57 appointments (senior managers required under the Municipal Systems Act). For each post, ask: Is the position filled permanently or by an acting official? Does the incumbent hold the minimum qualifications prescribed by regulation? How long have they been in the role? If more than 30% of senior technical posts are vacant, filled by acting officials, or held by people without prescribed qualifications, the capability element has collapsed.

**Test 2: The vacancy rate test.**

Look beyond senior management. Request the municipality's overall vacancy rate and the vacancy rate for technical positions specifically — engineers, accountants, town planners, environmental health officers, process controllers. A municipality can have a low overall vacancy rate (because non-technical posts are filled) while critical technical functions are hollowed out. The technical vacancy rate is the one that predicts service delivery failure.

**Test 3: The information availability test.**

Pick any municipal function — water, electricity, waste, roads — and try to find current performance data. Is the data published on the municipal website? Is it available through the municipal annual report? Can you find it through National Treasury or the relevant national department? If you cannot find basic operational data for a core function, the information element has failed — and oversight bodies cannot do their jobs either.

**Test 4: The Auditor-General trajectory test.**

Look at your municipality's audit outcomes over the last five years (available on the AGSA website). A municipality moving from unqualified to qualified to disclaimer audit opinions is on a capability decline trajectory. A municipality stuck on disclaimer for three or more years has experienced capability collapse — the institution cannot even account for how it spent public money.

**Test 5: The capture vulnerability test.**

For each of the five capture vectors — political, commercial, labour, regulatory, and information — ask whether structural safeguards exist. Are senior technical appointments protected from political interference? Is procurement data published openly? Are performance management systems actually enforced? If safeguards are absent on three or more vectors, the municipality is structurally vulnerable to capture regardless of who is in charge.

With these five tests, you can produce a capability diagnosis of your municipality that goes beyond anecdotes and identifies the specific structural gaps that need to be fixed — through legislation, regulation, or public advocacy.
:::
