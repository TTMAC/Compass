## The Bottom Line, Up Front

A staged civic system fails at its very first step: the transition out of school. This article sizes the post-matric cliff and argues it is a structural design gap, not a personal failing of young people.

## Outline

- **BLUF / hook** — The single day after the matric results: ~700,000–800,000 write matric, but the system has no organised next step for most. Open on a concrete figure and a named pathway gap.
- **The numbers (data-anchored)** — Youth unemployment (15–34) ~45% narrow / higher on the expanded definition; 15–24 unemployment far higher; NEET population (not in employment, education or training) ~3.4–4.5 million. *Sources to cite: Stats SA Quarterly Labour Force Survey (latest), DHET post-school statistics.*
- **The funnel** — From ~1.2m Grade 1 entrants to matric write-throughs to those who qualify for university/TVET to those who actually place. Show where young people fall out.
- **Why the cliff is structural** — No universal post-school destination; fragmented, opt-in programmes; geography and class determine who has a pathway. Distinguish "lazy youth" myth from system design.
- **The cost of idleness** — Links to crime, gangsterism, mental health, lost tax base, the "scarring" effect of early unemployment on lifetime earnings (cite labour economics evidence).
- **Reader's struggling moment** — "I matriculated and there's nothing for me / my child." Address directly.
- **Close with the practical tool** — A "post-matric pathway map" of what currently exists (TVET, learnerships, SA Youth / PYEI, NYDA) and its gaps — setting up Part 2's proposal.
