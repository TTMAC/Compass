# MECE Prompt Set: South Africa's Grand Strategy Article

**Target output:** One cohesive article of minimum 7,500 words
**Working title:** *Constitutional Non-Alignment for a Multipolar Era: South Africa's Grand Strategy — Analysis, Critique, and Realignment*

---

## How to Use These Prompts

Each prompt below corresponds to one **mutually exclusive section** of the article. Together they are **collectively exhaustive** of the article's scope. The prompts are sequenced in publication order — output from each can be concatenated into a single manuscript.

**Estimated word counts** are targets per section. Adjust upward on any prompt if the article is running short of 7,500 words.

---

## PROMPT 1 — Introduction & Conceptual Foundation
**Section:** *What Grand Strategy Is and Why It Matters for South Africa*
**Target:** ~800 words

```
Write the opening section (~800 words) of a long-form analytical article on South Africa's grand strategy.

This section must accomplish three things:
1. Define "grand strategy" for a general but educated audience — the highest level of statecraft where a nation coordinates all instruments of power (military, economic, diplomatic, technological, cultural) to secure core interests over decades. Distinguish it from military strategy ("how do we win this war?" vs "what kind of world do we want to live in?"). Reference the intellectual lineage: Liddell Hart, Paul Kennedy's thesis that great powers decline when strategic commitments outrun their economic base, Luttwak on Roman grand strategy evolving with circumstances, Gaddis, and Colin Gray.

2. Establish why grand strategy analysis matters specifically for South Africa right now — an upper-middle-income economy that is deindustrialising, trapped between the ambitions of a developed state (universal welfare, complex industry, global diplomacy) and the fiscal/institutional base of a struggling emerging market, with 32%+ unemployment, decayed infrastructure, and a newly published National Security Strategy (July 2025).

3. Preview the article's architecture: the piece will (a) reconstruct South Africa's actual grand strategy from its doctrinal stack, (b) evaluate it through multiple analytical lenses, (c) propose an endowment-aligned alternative, and (d) identify the gap between aspiration and execution.

Tone: rigorous, analytical, accessible to non-specialists. No bullet points — write in flowing prose with occasional subheadings. Do not use the word "delve."
```

## PROMPT 2 — The Doctrinal Stack
**Section:** *Reconstructing South Africa's Grand Strategy from Its Doctrinal Architecture*
**Target:** ~900 words

```
Write a section (~900 words) explaining that South Africa does not publish a single document titled "Grand Strategy." Instead, its grand strategy must be reconstructed from a layered doctrinal stack. Describe and contextualise each layer:

- **Constitutional tier:** Constitution (1996), especially §198, which defines security as inseparable from human dignity, equality, and freedom — making SA's grand strategy constitutionally values-bound in a way few states' strategies are.
- **Strategic tier:** The National Security Strategy (NSS) 2024–2029, publicly released 15 July 2025 — the first transparent publication of core intelligence doctrine in SA history, containing 8 security pillars. Also the National Intelligence Estimate (NIE) 2019–2024 and National Intelligence Priorities.
- **Foreign policy tier:** The White Paper "Building a Better World: The Diplomacy of Ubuntu" (2011); DIRCO 2025–2030 Strategic Plan; Framework Document on the National Interest (2022) with its four national interests (Survival, Prosperity, Identity, Influence).
- **Defence tier:** 1996 White Paper on Defence, 1998 & 2015 Defence Reviews, and the pending Long-Term Force Evolution Strategy 2025–2055 ("Journey to Greatness") still awaiting a Special NSC session as of December 2025.
- **Development tier:** National Development Plan (NDP) Vision 2030 as the domestic anchor.

Explain why the doctrinal stack approach matters analytically — and note the think-tank overlay (ISS, SAIIA, Brenthurst Foundation archive, Belfer Center, academic journals) that provides independent scrutiny.

Tone: analytical, prose-driven. No bullet-point lists in the output.
```

## PROMPT 3 — The Ends
**Section:** *What South Africa Seeks to Achieve: Vision, National Interests, and the Eight NSS Pillars*
**Target:** ~900 words

```
Write a section (~900 words) analysing the "Ends" — what South Africa's grand strategy seeks to achieve. Cover three nested layers:

1. **The overarching vision:** "A better South Africa, in a better Africa, in a better world" — a constitutionally-rooted, values-driven project treating domestic transformation, continental renewal, and reformed global order as one integrated programme. This formulation is repeated across the White Paper on Foreign Policy, the NDP, and DIRCO 2025–2030.

2. **The four core national interests** from the 2022 DIRCO Framework Document, reinforced by the NSS:
   - Survival (sovereignty, territorial integrity, constitutional order)
   - Prosperity (growth, jobs, reducing poverty/inequality/unemployment — "the triple challenge")
   - Identity (constitutional values, social cohesion, Ubuntu)
   - Influence (voice for Africa and the Global South in global governance)

3. **The eight NSS pillars** (2024–2029) as mutually exclusive security domains: Public Security; Territorial Integrity; Sovereignty Projection; Economic Security; Cyber & Information Security; Technology & Innovation; Environmental & Natural Resource Security; Cultural Security.

Also address the "negative" of the ends — the NIE's five threat axes (threats to the economy, territorial integrity, state authority, citizen well-being, and from the global environment) and how they map inversely onto the pillars.

Write analytically in prose. Assess whether the ends are internally coherent and whether they are realistic given SA's current economic position.
```

## PROMPT 4 — The Ways
**Section:** *Strategic Posture: How South Africa Converts Interests into Action*
**Target:** ~1,000 words

```
Write a section (~1,000 words) analysing the four distinctive "Ways" — the strategic concepts South Africa uses to convert interests into outcomes. Treat them as analytically separable though operationally interlinked:

1. **Active Non-Alignment / Strategic Autonomy:** Not neutrality but refusal of bloc allegiance combined with active coalition-building. Reference Minister Ronald Lamola's September 2025 articulation, the Hague Group co-chairmanship, the ICJ case against Israel as proof-points. Include the academic framing from Otto & Nagar (SAJIA 2025) of "negotiated institutional accommodation" — sustained through flexible legal interpretation rather than open defiance, as seen in the 2023 BRICS-summit Putin/ICC dilemma.

2. **Pan-African Agency & Regional Anchor Role:** Africa as doctrinally the "centre" of foreign/security policy. Operational expressions include: SADC/AU Standby Force support, SAMIDRC (DRC), AfCFTA championship, push for permanent African UNSC seat, peace-mediation credential projection.

3. **Ubuntu Diplomacy / Values-Based Norm Entrepreneurship:** The 2011 White Paper's diplomatic signature — humanity-as-interconnection, peaceful conflict resolution, Global South solidarity. Include the critique from Brooks Spector (via SAIIA) and ISS that it is normatively aspirational but operationally thin.

4. **Multi-Aligned Multilateralism:** Stacking memberships (BRICS, G20, IBSA, NAM, Commonwealth, AU, SADC, EU Strategic Partnership) to leverage every forum for Global South agenda-setting while maintaining Western market access. Reference the Belfer Center (Nov 2025) characterisation as "coordinated multi-alignment."

Assess whether these four ways are internally consistent or create contradictions (e.g., Ubuntu values vs authoritarian BRICS partners).
```

## PROMPT 5 — The Means
**Section:** *Instruments of National Power: The DIME Assessment*
**Target:** ~1,000 words

```
Write a section (~1,000 words) evaluating South Africa's instruments of national power using the DIME framework (Diplomatic, Informational, Military, Economic). This section must be honest about capability gaps.

**Diplomatic:** ~120 DIRCO missions abroad, 2025–2030 Strategic Plan centralising economic diplomacy, multilateral seats (G20, BRICS, rotational UNSC), convening power (2023 BRICS summit, 2025 G20 presidency, 2025 SA–EU summit), SADPA for development assistance.

**Informational:** Ubuntu narrative as soft power, G20 messaging on equitable international order, the NSS release itself as a transparency move. Weaknesses: cybersecurity posture, information-space sovereignty, counter-disinformation capability all explicitly under-developed (NSS Pillar 5).

**Military (SANDF) — the most degraded instrument:** Detail the structural crisis:
- Baseline budget requirement ~R98.3bn vs allocation ~R57.2bn (2025–26) = 43% structural deficit
- >60% of budget consumed by personnel costs
- Only ~12 of 26 Gripen fighters operational; C-130BZ transports not airworthy; submarine fleet non-functional; one operational frigate
- 14 SANDF soldiers killed in DRC (SAMIDRC) January 2025 exposing air-cover and ISR deficits
- Denel governance collapse
- The 2015 Defence Review's force-design trajectory was for Special Operations + Medium Combat Forces capability; current funding supports neither
- "Journey to Greatness" 2025–2055 targets 1.5% GDP defence floor that Minister Motshekga concedes will take "many years"

**Economic:** Strategic two-ocean geography, critical minerals (PGMs, manganese, chromium), ~$50bn BRICS trade (2022), dtic economic diplomacy mechanism. Vulnerabilities: FATF grey-listing, US 30% reciprocal tariff (Aug 2025), AGOA review, SOE decay.

Conclude with a clear judgement: the DIME portfolio is diplomatically overweight and militarily/economically hollow.
```

## PROMPT 6 — Perspectives & Contestation
**Section:** *Four Lenses: How Different Constituencies Read the Strategy*
**Target:** ~900 words

```
Write a section (~900 words) presenting four mutually exclusive interpretive camps and their readings of South Africa's grand strategy. This section ensures the article is analytically balanced, not just the government's view.

1. **Official Government Reading:** The NSS and DIRCO frame grand strategy as people-centred human security fused with active non-alignment. Minister Ntshavheni cast the July 2025 release as "democratic renewal." Success metrics: constitutional fidelity, African agency, reformed multilateralism, solving the triple challenge at home.

2. **Independent Think-Tank Mainstream (ISS, SAIIA):**
   - ISS (Jakkie Cilliers, African Futures): architecture is sound but undermined by foreign-policy drift toward Russia, DIRCO capacity decay, and failure to translate values into economic benefit. Foreign policy should be measured by trade, investment, growth — not rhetorical posture.
   - SAIIA (Elizabeth Sidiropoulos, G20 research coordinator): strategy is coherent on norm-entrepreneurship but suffers from inconsistency — moral rhetoric on Gaza outpacing action on Sudan and the Sahel. Recommends converting Global South numerical strength into agenda-setting power through technical expertise.

3. **Contrarian/Reformist Reading (Brenthurst Foundation archive, Belfer Center, DA/Free SA):**
   - Brenthurst (Greg Mills, Ray Hartley): grand-strategic failure is economic — without 5%+ growth, all strategic ambition is unfunded rhetoric. "Asian Aspiration" thesis: emulate East Asian developmental-state discipline.
   - Belfer Center (Nov 2025): SA is an ambitious Global South agenda-setter but China-leaning non-alignment has triggered US tariff retaliation.
   - DA critique: NSS is aspirational and bureaucratic — ambitious on paper, under-resourced, vague on trade-offs.

4. **Military/Defence-Community Reading (defenceWeb, Stellenbosch CMS, Africa Center for Strategic Studies):** NSS correctly identifies defence as a pillar but the SANDF cannot execute. Moses Khanyile highlights ISR and early-warning gaps. Africa Center (2019) argued the old classified NSS prevented coherent cross-departmental use; the 2025 publication partially remedies this.

Write as analytical prose with attribution. Do not editorially favour any camp — present each fairly and let the reader weigh them.
```

## PROMPT 7 — Risks & Implementation Gaps
**Section:** *The Delta Between Doctrine and Delivery: Six Failure Modes*
**Target:** ~800 words

```
Write a section (~800 words) identifying six mutually exclusive risk families — the gap between South Africa's stated strategy and observable execution.

1. **Ends–Means Mismatch:** The NSS asserts military power as a pillar; SANDF budget supports border patrol and limited peacekeeping — not regional anchor-state projection.

2. **Values–Interests Tension:** ICJ Gaza case, BRICS naval exercises with Russia/China, 2023 Putin/ICC dilemma. Otto & Nagar rate "Idealism under Strain" as the most probable scenario. Constitutional obligations (Rome Statute, human rights) vs strategic partnerships with authoritarian BRICS members.

3. **Coordination Deficits:** 2018 High-Level Review Panel on SSA identified policy incoherence. NSC was moribund before Ramaphosa's 2019 revival. NICOC, NSS Secretariat, NSC still face capacity questions.

4. **Economic Foundation Risk:** Grey-listing, stagnant growth, Eskom/Transnet dysfunction, 32%+ unemployment, capital outflows eroding the fiscal base on which every NSS pillar depends. This is the critique shared across all think-tank camps.

5. **External Coercion Risk:** US 30% reciprocal tariffs (Aug 2025), AGOA non-renewal threat, Trump administration bilateral review. Non-alignment becoming materially costly.

6. **Regional Blowback Risk:** Mozambique (Cabo Delgado), eastern DRC (M23), SADC fragmentation creating spillover exposure (terrorism, illicit arms, migration) that SANDF capability cannot address.

Tie the six risks together: they compound each other. Economic weakness starves the military, which undermines regional credibility, which reduces diplomatic leverage, which makes external coercion more effective.
```

## PROMPT 8 — The Alternative: An Endowment-Aligned Grand Strategy
**Section:** *Playing the Hand South Africa Actually Holds: A Prescriptive Grand Strategy*
**Target:** ~1,200 words

```
Write a section (~1,200 words) proposing an alternative grand strategy aligned to South Africa's actual stage of economic development and resource endowments. Frame this as what a best-practice, endowment-aligned strategy would look like if designed from first principles rather than inherited from doctrinal inertia.

Begin by defining the endowment stack SA should build around:
- World-leading reserves of platinum-group metals, manganese, chromium, vanadium — inputs for EVs, green hydrogen, grid batteries, stainless steel
- Deepest capital markets and most sophisticated financial/legal services sector in Africa
- Two-ocean geography astride the Cape route, natural logistics gateway to SADC
- Young population (demographic dividend open ~15–20 years) but severe skills mismatch
- Residual but decayed industrial, rail, and port infrastructure
- Diplomatic credibility as non-aligned middle power and BRICS member

Then present five mutually reinforcing pillars:

1. **Mineral-led green industrialisation:** Follow the Indonesia-nickel / Chile-lithium playbook — tie mining rights to domestic beneficiation in PGM fuel cells, manganese cathodes, vanadium flow batteries. The one sector where SA's endowments match 21st-century demand.

2. **Energy transition as industrial policy, not just decarbonisation:** Resolving the Eskom crisis as the binding constraint. Build domestic solar, wind, green-hydrogen manufacturing co-located with PGM deposits. Turn a liability into comparative advantage.

3. **Regional anchor strategy:** Be the financial, logistics, and industrial hub of AfCFTA. Johannesburg as Africa's capital market; Durban/Ngqura as container gateways. Leverage institutions, scale, and connectivity rather than competing head-on with Asia.

4. **State capacity reconstruction before expansion:** Ruthless civil service professionalisation, SOE reform (unbundling Eskom and Transnet), narrower but functional developmental state. Fiscal consolidation with targeted cash transfers, not universal BIG — the tax base cannot support Nordic ambitions.

5. **Strategic non-alignment as leverage:** Hedge between US/EU (market access, AGOA-type deals, capital), China (infrastructure, industrial capacity), BRICS (diplomatic weight). Extract concessions from all three without picking sides publicly.

Be explicit about what to give up: broad-spectrum manufacturing revival fantasies, import-substitution nostalgia, the pretence that SOEs can be both commercial and welfare instruments.

Flag the biggest internal risks: GNU coalition instability, patronage networks resisting SOE reform, and the inequality-growth tension where redistribution pressures outrun the productive base.
```

## PROMPT 9 — The Gap & Synthesis
**Section:** *Bridging the Gap: From Constitutional Non-Alignment to Endowment-Aligned Execution*
**Target:** ~700 words

```
Write a synthesising section (~700 words) that explicitly bridges the existing grand strategy (described in earlier sections) and the proposed alternative.

Argue that the current strategy's architecture is not wrong — its ends (NSS pillars), ways (non-alignment, Pan-Africanism, Ubuntu, multi-alignment), and values framework are broadly coherent. The problem is not doctrinal; it is a failure of economic foundation and execution priority.

Identify the three critical bridging moves:
1. **Reprioritise the economic security pillar** as first among equals — not one of eight co-equal pillars but the enabling condition for all others. Without 3–5% sustained GDP growth, the rest is performative.
2. **Ground diplomatic posture in commercial outcomes** — measure foreign policy by trade, FDI, and technology transfer, not by multilateral communiqué language. SAIIA's recommendation to convert numerical Global South strength into agenda-setting power through technical expertise applies here.
3. **Accept strategic triage on defence** — a 15-year roadmap to a modest but functional Special Operations + ISR + maritime-domain-awareness force, not a paper plan for a force the fiscus cannot fund.

Conclude that the dominant probable trajectory (per Otto & Nagar, Belfer, ISS) is sustained normative posture with incremental adaptation to external pressure and continued drift between stated and executable capability. The key variable is economic recovery — without it, grand strategy becomes performative.
```

## PROMPT 10 — Conclusion
**Section:** *Grand Strategy or Grand Illusion?*
**Target:** ~400 words

```
Write a concluding section (~400 words) for a long-form analytical article on South Africa's grand strategy.

Return to the Paul Kennedy thesis from the introduction: great powers decline when strategic commitments outrun their economic base. South Africa is not a great power, but the principle scales — a ~$400bn upper-middle-income economy with 32% unemployment cannot sustain the strategic ambition of a top-20 state without first rebuilding its productive foundation.

The July 2025 NSS represents genuine doctrinal progress — transparency, coherence, and a constitutionally grounded framework. But doctrine without economic capacity is aspiration, not strategy.

End with a forward-looking judgement: South Africa has approximately 15–20 years of demographic dividend remaining. The window for converting its mineral endowments into green-industrial advantage is narrowing as competitors move. The next five years will determine whether "Constitutional Non-Alignment for a Multipolar Era" becomes a functioning grand strategy or a well-written epitaph for strategic ambition that outran its means.

Tone: measured but pointed. No false optimism, no fatalism. The conclusion should leave the reader understanding both the potential and the urgency.
```
