# Entrepreneurial Opportunities

Products and services derived from the GovCompass content library (76 articles across 5 pillars).

---

## High-Confidence Opportunities

### 1. RAARICLE Diagnostic Toolkit (SaaS/Consulting)

The proprietary governance diagnostic framework is applied across multiple pillars. This could become:

- A licensed assessment tool for municipalities, civil society orgs, or donors to diagnose institutional failures
- Consulting engagements running RAARICLE diagnostics on specific service delivery chains
- Training workshops for civil society organisations

### 2. Municipal Performance Index (MPI) Dashboard

Articles eg-4.1 and eg-4.2 describe a quantitative ranking framework for 257 municipalities. This could be:

- A live, interactive dashboard (freemium model — free summary, paid detailed breakdowns)
- Sold as a data product to investors, development finance institutions, or NGOs assessing where to deploy resources
- A subscription service with quarterly updates tied to AGSA audit cycles

### 3. Civic Literacy Course / Certification

The 76-article series is essentially a structured curriculum. Package it as:

- A paid online course (self-paced or cohort-based) with quizzes, case studies, certificates
- CPD-accredited training for councillors, ward committee members, or public servants
- A "Civic Literacy Certificate" for corporate ESG/CSI programmes wanting to upskill employees or communities

### 4. Public Submission Writing Service

Article 4.3 is a step-by-step guide to writing public submissions. This maps to:

- A paid service helping citizens, ratepayer associations, or NGOs draft quality submissions on draft legislation, budgets, and municipal IDPs
- Templates + AI-assisted drafting tool based on the methodology

## Medium-Confidence Opportunities

### 5. "Follow the Money" Audit Interpretation Reports

Articles 2.1-2.3 and 5.1 teach people to read AGSA reports and trace fiscal flows. This could be:

- Plain-language municipal audit summaries sold to ratepayer associations, media houses, or opposition parties
- A subscription newsletter: "Your Municipality's Audit — Explained"

### 6. Ward Committee / Community Engagement Training

Articles 4.1-4.2 cover participation rights and ward committee mechanics. This becomes:

- In-person or virtual workshops for newly elected ward committee members (municipalities would pay for this — it's a compliance obligation they struggle with)
- A facilitator's guide sold to community development practitioners

### 7. Policy Brief Subscription for Corporates

The economic growth and reform pillars contain sophisticated policy analysis. Package as:

- Quarterly policy briefs for corporate affairs teams, industry associations, or foreign embassies needing to understand SA's governance landscape
- Priced like an analyst report (R5K-R25K/year per seat)

### 8. Speaker / Advisory Revenue

The depth across all five pillars positions the author as a subject-matter expert for:

- Conference speaking (governance, civic tech, public finance)
- Advisory boards for civic tech startups, development agencies, or local government associations

## Highest-Leverage Play

The **MPI Dashboard + RAARICLE Diagnostic** combination is the strongest moat — it's proprietary, data-driven, and addresses a gap no one else fills (accessible, citizen-facing municipal performance data with a structural diagnosis framework). The content already does 80% of the intellectual heavy lifting; the product is mostly a packaging and delivery problem.
